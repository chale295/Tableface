ABOUT TABLEFACE

Tableface is a party game invented at Haverford College. The virtual version you see here is a quarantine adaptation of the game, the original version of which requires only a large piece of cardboard, a bottle cap, and a group of willing participants. This version is made to be played over Zoom, with one person screensharing the board.


Here's how to play.


  1. Players (well, the screensharer has to do this on everyone's behalf) draw adjacent spaces on the board, and then add activities/challenges to each of the spaces as shown in the photo below. On the virtual board, you can toggle "Draw" and "Erase" modes in the bottom left corner of the screen. The activities can be as benign or as mischievous as the group desires - anything from "Choose a song to play over Zoom" to "Text your ex" is fair game. Make sure to include a large space in the center marked "TABLEFACE".

    You can keep adding to the board over the course of the game. Don't worry if there's unfilled space left on the board.

  2. A brave player begins the game by "flipping" the bottle cap onto a random position on the board and doing whatever activity the cap lands on. At any point, a player can opt out of the activity they land on and choose to TABLEFACE, putting their face down on the nearest table or surface until the next player unlucky enough to be TABLEFACED.

  3. Play proceeds circularly around the entire group, each player taking a turn flipping the cap and taking on whatever activity they land on. Spaces can be added, edited, or adjusted as the group desires. The game ends when everyone is sick of playing.
